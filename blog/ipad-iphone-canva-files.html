Where Your Designs Are Saved

How to pick your destination and find your files

Ever saved a design in Canva and then wondered what just happened? You are not alone. It can feel like tossing a paper airplane into a windy day and hoping to catch it later. In reality, your files have a clear path. You just need to know where to look.

Canva uses an internal folder—the app sandbox—to hold your working designs. This hidden space lives on your device. No other app can see it. You cannot browse it in the Files app. That keeps your drafts safe. It also means nothing appears there until you choose to export.

When you tap Share → Download, Canva packages your work into a new file. Pick a format. Choose where to save. Then a copy lands in a public spot. That spot might be on your iPad’s internal storage, a cloud drive, or even your Photos library. It all depends on your choice.

Below you will find a deep dive into each location. You will learn how to set default save spots and how to track down that file when it seems to vanish. No code. No developer tools. Just the Files app, a few taps, and common sense.

Canva’s Private Folder

Every iOS and Android app lives in a sandbox. Think of it as a private cabinet. Canva stores project files here. These files include SVG data, PNG previews, JSON that describes your layout, and more. They remain isolated. You cannot export them directly. You only see thumbnails in the Canva interface.

This design vault protects your work. It prevents accidental deletion and keeps intermediate versions intact. You can work offline. Edits happen instantly. But that data never shows up in your Photos or Files until you ask.

The Export Copy

Exporting makes a fresh copy. Canva leaves the original project untouched. You can export multiple times. Each time, you get a new file. That is why you see version 1, version 2, or version final. The master stays safe.

Short answer: Export equals copy. Copy goes where you want. The original never moves.

Selecting a Save Location

After you tap Download, a menu appears. You will see options like:

  • On My iPad/iPhone — a folder in the Files app
  • iCloud Drive — in your cloud storage
  • Photos — straight to your camera roll
  • Other Clouds — Dropbox, Google Drive, OneDrive if added
  • Email or Messages — attach and send directly

Pick one. Tap Save. It feels obvious when you do it. Yet newcomers often miss that final confirmation. If you leave the picker without tapping Save, nothing moves.

Offline Versus Online Spots

Saving to On My iPad works offline. No internet needed. That is your local folder. Good for big files or when you lack signal. However, those files only exist on that device.

Saving to iCloud Drive needs connectivity. Once done, your file syncs to all Apple devices on your account. You can reach it in Finder on a Mac or via the iCloud for Windows app. It shows up in the Files app under iCloud Drive.

Naming and Organizing

Files tend to pile up if you do not organize early. Create a folder called Canva Exports. Add subfolders by project or date. For example:


$ mkdir -p "iCloud Drive/Canva Exports/EventFlyers/2025-05"

Use clear names: ConferencePoster_v2.png. Then use the search field in Files to locate it fast.

Using Tags

Files supports tags on iOS. You can add colored dots or custom labels. Tag urgent exports in red. Tag drafts in yellow. Tags show up across locations, making it easy to filter.

Third-Party Cloud Drives

Want Dropbox or Google Drive? Install the official app. Then go to Files → Edit in the three-dot menu. Toggle the cloud you need. It appears as a location. Now, when exporting, pick Save to Files, open your Dropbox folder, and tap Save.

Searching Like a Pro

Tapping the search bar in Files is often fastest. Type part of your file name or extension. You will see results across all enabled locations. It saves time versus browsing nested folders.

Quick Troubleshooting Steps

Simple checks to locate missing exports

Can’t find your file? Start here. These steps cover the majority of hiccups.

1. Enable the Right Location

Open Files. Tap three-dot menu. Choose Edit. Enable iCloud Drive, Dropbox, Google Drive or any cloud you need. Now the folder appears.

2. Confirm the Save Action

In Canva’s share sheet make sure you tapped Save after selecting Save to Files. It only moves when you hit that final button.

3. Check the Correct Folder

Look under the location you chose. New exports often land in a Downloads folder or a Canva folder inside it. Open each to check.

4. Verify Connectivity

Saving to the cloud needs online access. Open Settings and confirm Wi-Fi or cellular is on. Then open Files and tap the file name. That forces a sync.

5. Restart Apps or Device

Swipe the app away in the switcher. Relaunch. Still broken? Power cycle your device. A fresh start clears most minor glitches.

6. Update Software

Open the App Store. Tap your profile. Update iOS, Canva, and any cloud app. Bug fixes roll out in updates and often resolve picker errors.

7. Test a Small File

Try exporting a single small PNG. If it shows up, the export path is fine and you can adjust bigger exports later.

8. Reinstall as Last Resort

Delete and reinstall Canva or the cloud app. It clears corrupted caches and resets permissions. Re-enable the service in Files → Edit afterward.

Stay Calm and Carry On

By following these checks you save time and frustration. You will know exactly where your files are and why they might not appear. That frees you to focus on design, not detective work.
